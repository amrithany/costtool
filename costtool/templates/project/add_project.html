My Project

To execute a "cost analysis" you will be asked to enter information about each “ingredient” or resource needed to implement one or more educational programs/interventions. You will be guided to assign prices to each ingredient and CostOut will estimate the costs of the program(s) for you.

You can consider “Total costs” of the program or only the “Incremental Costs,” i.e., the additional costs of your program above a baseline such as business as usual or a comparison condition.

A "cost-effectiveness analysis" compares two or more programs. For each program, you will need to provide information about the program’s effectiveness relative to a comparison condition and the ingredients or resources used above and beyond what is used for the comparison condition (i.e., the incremental costs). Costs should be estimated for the same period of time over which the effectiveness is reported in order to reflect the ingredients required to elicit the observed level of effectiveness over that time period.
